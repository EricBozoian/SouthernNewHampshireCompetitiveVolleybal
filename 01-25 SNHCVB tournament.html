Tournament Description:

This tournament is designed for players who have solid volleyball skills but aren’t playing at an elite level. If you’re comfortable with the basics like passing, serving, spiking, and understanding rotations—but you’re not dominating every play or consistently performing advanced techniques—you’ll fit right in. The focus is on Intermediate to Advanced-level competition, which means competitive yet balanced games.

What Players Skill Level Is Too High for This Tournament?

Players at the Advanced Plus (AP) level or higher are not eligible. If you can consistently execute high-powered, strategically placed serves, dominate the net with precise blocking, or seamlessly adjust to complex plays under intense pressure, this event is not the right fit for you. We aim to ensure a level playing field for all participants, so players with elite-level performance are encouraged to join events designed for their skill set.

Who Is A Good Fit?

If your skills include solid passing, consistent spiking, and an understanding of rotations—without the ability to consistently overpower opponents with advanced plays—then you’re perfect for this tournament. Players in this range typically have reliable fundamentals and good game awareness but aren’t dominating every play.

Player Skill Level Limits

Teams made up of any combination of skill levels from Novice (N) to Advanced (A) are allowed to play. Players that are considered Advanced Plus (AP) to Expert Plus (XP) are not allowed to play.

General Rules:

All SNHCVB events are governed by the Southern New Hampshire Competitive Volleyball (SNHCVB) Rules 2024-2025. These rules cover indoor volleyball, co-ed play, RCO, and grass volleyball, and emphasize fairness, respect, and competitive integrity. The flexibility of these rules allows us to adapt to local needs and tournament formats, ensuring smooth operation at every event. Tournament-specific rules are provided in the event descriptions, with general guidelines applying unless otherwise specified.
